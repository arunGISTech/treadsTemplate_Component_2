A Brief Timeline for the Peddavagu Reservoir Project

  1. 1969

    Central Government constituted the Godavari Water Dispute Tribunal to resolve differences between then riparian states of Karnataka, Maharashtra, United Madhya Pradesh (later bifurcated into MP and CG in 2001), United Andhra Pradesh (bifurcated into AP and TS in 2014), and Orissa.

  2. 1975

    Agreement reached between the riparian states for sub-basin-wise water allocation. Based on the agreement water utilization from the Peddavagu project which is situated in the G-10 basin came entirely under the jurisdiction of the United A.P.

    Construction of the Peddavagu project started by united Andhra Pradesh under the V-FYP.

  3. 1979

    Report of the GWDT. The GWDT in its decision allocated water to the riparian states agreement dated 19th of December 1975

  4. 1983

    Peddavagu project was completed in 1983 at a cost of Rs. 810 lakhs is to serve an ayacut of 16,000 acres in the erstwhile Andhra Pradesh.

  5. 1985

    High Flood and breach of embankment in the Peddavagu project.

  6. 2014

    The A.P. Reorganization Act was passed in parliament in 2014. The Act lists out the specifics involved in the process of bifurcation constituting Telangana and Andhra Pradesh as a separate state. With the bifurcation, Peddavagu turned into an inter-state project with its headworks in Telangana, and greater proportion of irrigation benefits to Andhra Pradesh.
